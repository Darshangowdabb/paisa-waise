Filing your first income tax return (ITR) can feel scary. What form do I use? What documents do I need? Will I get a notice if I make a mistake? 

Take a breath — it's simpler than you think. This guide will walk you through the entire process from scratch.

## Do you even need to file ITR?

If your annual income is above ₹2.5 lakh (under the old tax regime) or ₹3 lakh (under the new tax regime), you must file ITR. Even if your employer deducted TDS and your tax liability is zero, filing ITR is still a good idea — it acts as income proof for loans and visas.

## What documents you need

Before you start, gather these:
- **Form 16** — your employer will give you this by June 15
- **PAN card** and **Aadhaar card**
- **Bank account details** (account number and IFSC)
- **Investment proofs** — if you invested in ELSS, PPF, LIC etc. for Section 80C deduction

## Which ITR form should freshers use?

As a salaried employee with income only from salary and savings bank interest:

**Use ITR-1 (Sahaj)** — It's the simplest form, designed exactly for people like you.

If you have capital gains from selling mutual funds or stocks, use ITR-2 instead.

## Step-by-step filing process

### Step 1: Login to the Income Tax Portal

Go to [incometax.gov.in](https://incometax.gov.in) and log in using your PAN as your user ID. If it's your first time, register with your PAN.

### Step 2: Start a new ITR

Go to **e-File → Income Tax Returns → File Income Tax Return**. Select Assessment Year 2025-26 (for income earned in FY 2024-25).

### Step 3: Choose ITR-1

Select ITR-1 and choose **Online mode**. The portal will pre-fill a lot of your data from Form 26AS and your employer's filing.

### Step 4: Verify pre-filled data

Check that your salary details match your Form 16. If your employer filed correctly, most fields will already be filled.

### Step 5: Add deductions under Section 80C

If you invested in any of these during the year, claim your deductions:
- **PPF contributions** — up to ₹1.5L
- **ELSS mutual funds** — up to ₹1.5L
- **LIC premium** — up to ₹1.5L
- **EPF** — usually auto-filled from salary

The maximum deduction under 80C is ₹1.5 lakh.

### Step 6: Compute your tax

The portal calculates your tax automatically. If TDS was deducted correctly by your employer, your tax payable will be ₹0 or you may even get a refund.

### Step 7: Verify and submit

After reviewing, click **Submit**. Then verify your return — the easiest way is using **Aadhaar OTP** (instant). You can also use net banking or send a physical ITR-V to CPC Bangalore.

## When is the deadline?

For salaried employees, the ITR deadline is typically **July 31**. File before this to avoid a ₹1,000–5,000 late filing fee.

## Will you get a tax refund?

If your employer deducted more TDS than your actual tax liability (which happens if you declared your investments late in the year), you'll get a refund. Refunds usually come within 20–45 days of verification directly to your bank account.
