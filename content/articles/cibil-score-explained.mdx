Your CIBIL score is a three-digit number between 300 and 900 that tells lenders how trustworthy you are with borrowed money. A score above 750 opens doors — lower interest rates, faster loan approvals, better credit card offers. A score below 650 can get you rejected outright.

Here's everything you need to know.

## What is a good CIBIL score?

| Score Range | Rating | What it means |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| 750 – 900 | Excellent | Best rates, instant approvals |
| 700 – 749 | Good | Most loans approved |
| 650 – 699 | Fair | Approved with conditions |
| 550 – 649 | Poor | Often rejected |
| 300 – 549 | Very Poor | Almost always rejected |

Aim for 750+. That's the magic number most banks use as a cutoff for their best products.

## What affects your CIBIL score?

**Payment history (35%)** — The biggest factor. Even one missed EMI or credit card payment can drop your score by 50–100 points. Always pay on time.

**Credit utilisation (30%)** — How much of your credit limit you're using. Keep it below 30%. If your card limit is ₹1 lakh, don't use more than ₹30,000 regularly.

**Length of credit history (15%)** — Older accounts help. Don't close your oldest credit card even if you don't use it much.

**Credit mix (10%)** — Having a mix of secured (home loan, car loan) and unsecured (credit card, personal loan) credit helps.

**New credit inquiries (10%)** — Every time you apply for a loan or card, a "hard inquiry" happens and temporarily reduces your score. Don't apply to 5 banks at once.

## How to check your CIBIL score (free)

1. Go to [cibil.com](https://www.cibil.com) or use apps like Paytm, BankBazaar, or CRED
2. You get one free report per year from CIBIL directly
3. Apps like CRED give free monthly updates

Check your score every 3–6 months to catch any errors early.

## How to improve a bad score — step by step

### If your score is below 650:

**Step 1: Pay all overdue amounts immediately.** Missed payments are the #1 reason for a bad score. Clear all pending EMIs and credit card dues now.

**Step 2: Don't miss another payment — set auto-pay.** Even minimum payment on your credit card counts. Set auto-pay for at least the minimum amount so you never miss again.

**Step 3: Get a secured credit card.** If you don't have a credit card, apply for a secured card (backed by an FD). Use it for small purchases and pay in full every month. Your score will start improving in 3–6 months.

**Step 4: Reduce credit card utilisation.** If you're using 80% of your card limit, pay it down to under 30%. This can improve your score within one billing cycle.

**Step 5: Don't apply for new credit.** Stop applying for loans or cards for at least 6 months. Each application adds a hard inquiry that temporarily hurts your score.

## How long does it take to fix?

With consistent effort, here's a realistic timeline:
- **0–30 days** — Pay off overdue amounts, utilisation drops
- **3–6 months** — Score starts climbing noticeably
- **12–18 months** — From a poor score to a good one (650+)
- **24–36 months** — From poor to excellent (750+)

There are no shortcuts. Anyone promising to "fix your CIBIL score in 7 days" is a scammer.

## Common CIBIL myths

**Myth: Checking your own score hurts it.** False — checking your own score is a "soft inquiry" and has zero impact.

**Myth: Debit card usage builds credit.** False — debit cards don't appear on your credit report at all.

**Myth: Closing a credit card improves your score.** Usually false — closing old cards can hurt your score by reducing your total credit limit and shortening your credit history.
