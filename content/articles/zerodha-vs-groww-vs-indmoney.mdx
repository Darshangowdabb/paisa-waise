Three apps dominate India's retail investing space: Zerodha, Groww, and INDmoney. They're all SEBI-registered, all reasonably safe, and all let you invest in mutual funds and stocks. So how do you choose?

The answer depends on what kind of investor you are.

## Quick comparison

| Feature | Zerodha | Groww | INDmoney |
|---------|---------|-------|----------|
| Best for | Active traders + investors | Beginners | All-in-one tracking |
| Stocks | Yes (Kite) | Yes | Yes |
| Mutual Funds | Yes (Coin) | Yes | Yes |
| US Stocks | No | No | Yes |
| Account opening fee | ₹200 | Free | Free |
| Annual maintenance | ₹300/yr | Free | Free |
| UI complexity | Medium | Simple | Medium |
| Research tools | Excellent | Basic | Good |

## Zerodha — best for active investors

Zerodha is India's largest discount broker by number of active clients. If you want to actively trade stocks, derivatives, or need serious research tools, Zerodha's Kite platform is unmatched.

**Pros:**
- Best charting and technical analysis tools in India
- Zerodha Coin for zero-commission direct mutual funds
- Highly reliable — been around since 2010
- Varsity app for free financial education (genuinely excellent)

**Cons:**
- ₹200 account opening fee
- ₹300/year demat maintenance charge
- The Kite app can overwhelm complete beginners

**Choose Zerodha if:** You want to invest in both stocks and mutual funds, and you're willing to spend a little time learning the platform.

## Groww — best for beginners

Groww is the most beginner-friendly investing app in India. It launched in 2016 targeting millennials who found traditional platforms complicated, and it shows in every design decision.

**Pros:**
- Zero fees — no account opening, no annual maintenance
- Extremely clean and simple UI
- Good selection of mutual funds with clear performance data
- Quick KYC — usually done in under 10 minutes

**Cons:**
- Research tools are basic compared to Zerodha
- Customer support can be slow during peak times
- Limited advanced order types for traders

**Choose Groww if:** This is your first investment app and you want something that feels like Instagram — simple, visual, and friendly.

## INDmoney — best for tracking everything

INDmoney is less of a brokerage and more of a financial superapp. It connects to your salary account, tracks all your investments across platforms, shows your net worth in real time, and also lets you invest in US stocks — something Zerodha and Groww don't offer.

**Pros:**
- Tracks everything in one place — salary, bank, MF, stocks, US stocks, EPF
- US stock investing (Apple, Google, Tesla) from ₹1
- Good for people with investments scattered across platforms
- Free to use

**Cons:**
- More features = more complexity
- US stock investing involves currency risk and additional taxes
- Newer platform compared to Zerodha

**Choose INDmoney if:** You already invest in multiple places and want a single dashboard, or you want to buy US stocks.

## Our recommendation

**New to investing?** Start with Groww. Open your account, do KYC, start a ₹500 SIP in a Nifty 50 index fund. Don't overthink it.

**Want to also trade stocks?** Go with Zerodha. The slightly higher cost is worth it for the superior tools.

**Want to invest in US markets or see your full financial picture?** Add INDmoney alongside whichever platform you're already on.

There's no rule that says you can only use one app. Many Indians use Groww for mutual funds and Zerodha for stocks — perfectly fine.

## Safety — are they all safe?

Yes. All three are:
- Registered with SEBI
- Required by law to keep your money in a separate client account
- Covered under SEBI's investor protection framework

Your mutual fund investments are held by the fund house directly (not by the app), so even if an app shuts down, your money is safe.
